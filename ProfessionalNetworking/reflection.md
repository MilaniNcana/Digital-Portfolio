# Reflection: Professional Networking (STAR Technique)

## S – Situation
I was required to complete a formative assessment on **Professional Networking** as part of the work readiness training program. The assessment focused on key networking concepts, including the primary purpose of professional networking, understanding networking event terminology, applying the *golden rule* of networking, identifying common networking mistakes, and outlining the first steps in building a professional network. The assessment was completed with a **Final Mark of 100/100**.

## T – Task
My task was to demonstrate a comprehensive theoretical understanding of effective professional networking strategies. This involved correctly identifying and applying principles required to build and maintain meaningful professional relationships, which are essential for career development and job-seeking.

## A – Action
I studied the networking module in detail, focusing on the strategic and ethical aspects of professional networking, particularly the *golden rule* and best practices for initiating professional relationships. I paid close attention to differentiating effective networking behaviours from common mistakes. During the assessment, I approached each question systematically, carefully analysing the scenarios presented. Where questions required reconsideration, I reviewed the content and adjusted my responses accordingly, ensuring full alignment with the core networking principles taught.

## R – Result
The assessment was completed with a **Final Mark of 100/100**, providing clear evidence of my understanding of professional networking fundamentals. Through this process, I demonstrated the ability to:

- Articulate the primary purpose of professional networking  
- Apply appropriate etiquette, including the *golden rule* of networking  
- Identify and avoid common networking mistakes  
- Strategically outline the first steps in building a professional network  

This knowledge equips me to engage confidently and professionally in networking opportunities, supporting long-term career growth and employability.
